Flowers

Flowers are the reproductive structures of flowering plants, designed to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. They showcase a diverse range of colors, shapes, and scents, each adapted to their specific pollinator. Petals, sepals, stamens (male parts), and pistils (female parts) make up a flower's anatomy. They play vital roles in plant reproduction, leading to seed and fruit development. Beyond their biological function, flowers hold symbolic meanings across cultures, representing beauty, love, and celebration. They enrich our environment, providing aesthetic pleasure and supporting ecosystems.
